# UI-UX-Experiment5

## Aim:

The UX Design Experiment aims to address critical user experience issues in two real-world digital design contexts:

Health & Wellness App Design:
The goal is to design a user-friendly mobile application that helps users prioritize and sustain healthy habits by reducing confusion from multiple goals and providing clear, actionable, and personalized guidance. The app should improve clarity, motivation, and consistency in daily routines.

Government Service Portal Redesign:
The goal is to revamp an outdated and complex government web portal used for services such as license renewal and vehicle registration. The redesigned portal should focus on improving navigation, simplifying language, reducing form complexity, and enhancing accessibility for non-technical users and senior citizens.

## Algorithm:

Identify user pain points through secondary research methods such as online surveys, app store reviews, and website feedback. Analyze user complaints and behavior patterns. Define UX problem statements based on specific user needs. Choose suitable research methods:

Health App: Online Surveys, App Review Analysis, Usability Testing
Gov Portal: Website Review Analysis, Heuristic Evaluation, Surveys

Create user personas to represent typical users and their goals. Use insights to inform design improvements for usability, personalization, and accessibility.

### Scenario 1: Health & Wellness App

Pain Point: Lack of prioritization, unclear progress tracking, and non-actionable suggestions.

#### Problem Statements:
Users trying to maintain healthy habits need a way to prioritize daily wellness activities because multiple equal goals create confusion, which results in low motivation and inconsistent routines.

Users need a way to receive clear and actionable health guidance because generic recommendations do not fit their lifestyle, which results in disengagement from the app.
Research Insights (Secondary Research).

Personas helped guide feature prioritization and improve user engagement.

### Scenario 2: Government Service Portal

Pain Point: Complex navigation, long forms, and lack of user control.

#### Problem Statements:

Citizens need a way to complete government services efficiently because complex navigation and unclear instructions create confusion, which results in incomplete applications.

Users need a way to control their input during processes because rigid and non-editable systems restrict actions, which results in frustration and task abandonment.

## Output:

#### Health & Wellness App

Identified key challenges such as lack of prioritization, unclear progress tracking, and ineffective suggestions. Designed features that:

• Introduce priority-based daily goals (Top 3 focus tasks)

• Provide clear progress indicators (streaks, completion bars)

• Deliver adaptive and realistic recommendations

• Reduce clutter with a simplified and focused interface

Discovered major usability gaps including:

• Difficult navigation and unclear steps

• Long and complex forms

• Limited user control 

#### Government Service Portal

Redesign included:

• Simplified user journeys with fewer steps

• Editable fields and better user control

• Plain-language instructions

• Improved accessibility for elderly and non-technical users

Personas ensured diverse user needs were addressed.
Personas helped guide feature prioritization and improve user engagement.

## Result:

Health App: Improved focus and motivation by prioritizing tasks and simplifying UI.
Gov Portal: Reduced confusion and improved completion rates through better usability.

Overall: Increased user satisfaction, reduced frustration, and improved task success.
